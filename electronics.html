Coil Gun

March 1st, 2024

This is the schematic for my coilgun. The battery cell consists of 18650 rechargeable lithium ion batteries. I use 4 of them, each
rated at 3.7V 9900 mAh, which adds up to around 15 volts. When KEY1 is pressed, this voltage is stepped up by the booster module 
to 400V, which charges the capacitor. I installed a cool little LED indicator to visualize the capacitor charging. In the center of 
the coil, which consists of 18 gauge wire wound tightly around a spool, I load my janky ammunition. I literally sawed off some ferro
-magnetic nails that were fat enough to fit in the barrel snugly. Depressing the "Push Button" will discharge the capacitor of all  
it's electrons into the coil which will induce a high magnetic field, sending our ferrous spike sailing. The purpose of the thyristor 
was just as a safer HV switch, A thyristor acts like a MOSFET (but with current on the gate instead of voltage.) When the trigger 
button is pushed, a current from the AAA battery switches the discharging loop closed. This avoids any potential switch failure 
due to HV and also prevents any injury to the user by hiding away the HV from the main interface.
